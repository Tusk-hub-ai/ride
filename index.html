03 / The rules

Clear for everyone, up front

01

Every funded kilometre gets ridden

Whatever the ladder funds, I ride — logged on a bike computer and tracked by satellite, so it's verifiable.

02

The ladder is fixed once I roll out

The rates above are locked the day I leave. No quiet changes mid-ride to bands that have already been set.

03

If donations slow within a tier

I keep the option to adjust the rate for the distance that's left, or to finish at the kilometre that's been funded — and I'll say so openly here if it happens.

04

The money never touches me

You donate directly to World Bicycle Relief through their own page. I never handle a cent — I just ride for them.

Offline, but trackable

I'm going dark on purpose.

A phone with no SIM card, so I'm offline the whole way. Navigation comes off a bike computer; there are no daily posts and no scrolling.

The one thread to the outside world is a Garmin inReach Mini 2 satellite communicator — it pings my position over the Iridium network and carries an SOS button if anything goes badly wrong. That same signal is what lets you follow the dot, and it's what makes every funded kilometre something you can verify rather than take on trust.

~€150funds one bicycle

World Bicycle Relief builds rugged Buffalo Bicycles for people in rural regions, then gets them to students, health workers, and farmers who'd otherwise be on foot for hours a day.

A student reaches a school that was too far. A nurse covers more villages. A farmer gets crops to market before they spoil. Every ~€150 raised here is, roughly, one of those bikes — and the full route adds up to about fifteen of them.

That's the asymmetry that made me pick them: the same machine carrying me across a continent for the fun of it is, for someone else, the difference between a long walk and a short ride to class.
